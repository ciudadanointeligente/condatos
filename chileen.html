About Mexico

Mexico City is a vibrant metropolis bubbling with both history and innovation. In the last years it has become a hub for social and technological entrepreneurship while remaining a center for finance, politics and culinary arts. Below are some guidelines to enjoy the city.

Transporte

Flights to Mexico City from every major airport in the world.

Public transport in Mexico City is not very tourist-friendly. We recommend not catching cabs from the street. The alternative is to call on certified taxis from the restaurant or hotel. Another option is to use Taxi services via mobile app. We have Uber, Cabify, and Yaxi. We recommend that you download them from your hometown so you can use them whenever you get here without using up all your data on roaming. From the airport: There are certified taxis at the airport. You can easily purchase a ticket from any of their booths to take you to your hotel.

Migration

If you’re a national of a country who needs a visa to enter Mexico you can be exempt if you:

a) Have a valid visa for the United States of America

b) Present a Card that credits you as a Permanent Resident in Canada, USA, Japan, UK, or any country within the Schengen Area.

To verify if you need a visa or not to come to Mexico you can check this link.

Accomodation

You want to stay close to the City Center - where ConDatos and all side events will take place. Cool neighborhoods close to ConDatos are Centro, Roma Norte, Juarez and Condesa (from closest to furthest). The most trendy are Roma and Condesa. Juarez is right by Reforma, Mexico’s main road, and lies conveniently close to all venues. Centro is the historic center and is close to the main plaza, cathedral and innumerable museums.

Security

Mexico City has received a lot of negative press in terms of security. In reality, it’s not such a dangerous place. Like in any big city, you need be cautious. Especially when you’re a tourist. ConDatos and all it’s side events are within the city center. Try to stay within the neighborhoods discussed above, follow the guidelines for transportation and most of all use your common sense. You’ll find that Mexico is welcoming and fun.

Currency

You can exchange Mexican Pesos for your local currency at the airport, hotels, bancs, and at foreign exchange offices. To skip that process you can also go to an ATM and withdraw money if you have a Visa or Mastercard. Consult your bank about the service surcharge. It’s normally worth the hassle and you get a pretty decent exchange rate. Banks are open from 9 am to 4 pm.

Credit and debit cards are widely accepted in commercial establishments, however, cash will be necessary for smaller establishments.

Electricity

Electric currrent in Mexico City is 110 volts. We use type A and B outlets (see image). If you have an electric device that uses different voltage, you will need an adapter.
